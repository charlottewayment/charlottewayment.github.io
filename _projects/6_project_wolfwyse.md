**Role:** Python Software & ML Engineer (Part-Time; Full-Time during holidays)  
**Period:** 2021 – 2025  
**Category:** Professional

From 2021 to 2025, alongside my undergraduate and postgraduate studies, I worked as a Python software and ML engineer at Wolfwyse Ltd., designing and deploying optimisation systems for large-scale manufacturing environments. Clients operated across food and pharmaceutical industries — including MARS and GSK — processing multiple millions of pounds of goods each week across multinational facilities.

The core challenge in these settings are the highly constrained, interdependent processes operating under real-world uncertainty, where a poor scheduling decision has immediate and costly downstream consequences - all familiar challenges from a systems perspective. The work required translating the complex operational realities of resource contention, sequencing dependencies, regulatory constraints, and failure modes into tractable computational models.

The primary output was a production-grade scheduling algorithm that reduced multi-day manual planning tasks to minutes, formalising operational constraints, dependencies, and resource flows into a computational model. This was developed far beyond an academic prototype, being integrated into internal APIs and deployed in live production environments, with automated feasibility, constraint, and regression checks built into the pipeline. The broader optimisation framework combined constraint-based methods with evolutionary algorithms, enabling robust scenario evaluation under real-world manufacturing conditions.

This engagement was formative for the direction of my research. Working in safety- and reliability-critical production environments, where a system bug has immediate real-world consequences, and where "it works in testing" is not enough, shaped my approach in building software that is reproducible, verifiable, and deployable rather than merely demonstrably performant - as is often the criticism in academic settings.
