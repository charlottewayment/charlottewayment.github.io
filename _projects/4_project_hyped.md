**Role:** Simulations Engineer (Academic)  
**Period:** 2023 – 2024  
**Category:** Academic

As simulations engineer for the [University of Edinburgh Hyperloop Team (HYPED)](https://hyped.co.uk/), I supported the development of a working Hyperloop prototype for European Hyperloop Week — an interdisciplinary student engineering competition.

My role focused on multi-scale CFD and solid mechanics simulations using [COMSOL Multiphysics](https://www.comsol.com/), that was used to inform structural design decisions and identify high-risk failure points in the electromechanical system before physical prototyping. Simulation-informed iteration reduced manufacturing waste by more than 30% compared to the previous development cycle.

The work required bridging fluid dynamics, structural mechanics, and manufacturing constraints within tight iteration cycles and, crucially, used simulation outputs to make principled decisions about where the system was most likely to fail. Thatframing, where modelling was used to understand failure modes and design around them rather than simply validate expected behaviour, has remained a consistent thread through subsequent work.
